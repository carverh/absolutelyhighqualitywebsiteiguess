artists statement

This is a lowpoly picture of me. It is based on my 2020 school photo. Added to it is a crude drawing of a cloud (thinking bubble) with a sun, clouds, and house. The name of this work is either img/poly.png, file:///home/carver/Documents/poly.png, , or https://carverh.github.io/absolutelyhighqualitywebsiteiguess/img/poly.png depending on where it is located.

I made this using a scanner, GIMP, and lowpoly creation software. I scanned my school photo, then I lowpoly'd it, then I resized and cleaned it up using GIMP and converted it to PNG and JPG.

The message of my work is how I feel going back to school. No more waking up at 12pm, no more sun which I stay inside for, no more gaming. All of that is lost. This work says a lot about our society in my honest opinion. It shows the pain and suffering students have to go through when they go back to school, in a cycle which lasts 12 years

My goal was to make this artpiece in TempleOS due to its unique art style. However, I did not successfully do this because of many issues. First was that it turns out that 640x480 is a very bad resolution to edit a picture at. It also turns out that the VGA 16-color pallate has only 6 colors that can reasonably be used for skin and hair (BLACK, WHITE, LTGRAY, DKGRAY, YELLOW, BROWN) with WHITE, LTGRAY, and DKGRAY all being shades of BLACK, and YELLOW being the bright shade of BROWN. Even with dithering which brought the color count up, it just looked really bad. Also, there were outlines that couldn't be removed and moving a point was slow and required mouse skills I don't have. I should have seen this coming when I tried to use image editing software designed by an insane person.

I leaned that I should not use image editing softwaer designed by insane people and instead use the supperior software, mspaint.exe, specifically the Windows 2000 and Windows 7 versions.
